# World Bank World Development Indicators (WDI)
## Overview
The World Bank World Development Indicators (WDI) dataset is one of the most comprehensive collections of global development data. It provides economic, social, environmental, governance, infrastructure, and demographic indicators collected from countries around the world.

This dataset serves as the primary tabular dataset for our project and provides quantitative indicators required to assess city and regional development.

## Dataset Statistics

- Records: 17,272
- Features: 50
- Countries/Regions: 274
- Time Period: 1960–2023

## Key Features Used

### Infrastructure

- Electric Power Consumption
- Internet Usage

### Economy
- GDP
- Population Density

### Environment
- CO₂ Emissions
- Renewable Energy Consumption
- Forest Area
- Agricultural Land

### Health
- Government Health Expenditure
- Life Expectancy

### Education
- Government Education Expenditure

### Governance
- Government Effectiveness
- Rule of Law
- Control of Corruption

## Why We Selected This Dataset

This dataset provides internationally recognized development indicators that directly measure multiple dimensions of urban and national development. It is maintained by the World Bank, making it a reliable and trustworthy source for research.


## Role in Our Project

This dataset will provide socioeconomic, environmental, governance, and infrastructure indicators that will be integrated with satellite imagery and environmental datasets to support City Development Index prediction.
